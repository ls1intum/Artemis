# Iris

Iris is an AI-powered virtual tutor integrated directly into Artemis. It provides context-aware support for programming exercises, text exercises, and lecture content, helping students learn independently through guided hints and explanations rather than giving away solutions. Iris is also available as a course-wide assistant for general course-related questions.

## Getting Started

### Choosing Your AI Experience

When you first open Iris, you choose how your interactions are processed:

- **Cloud** (Recommended): Uses cutting-edge cloud models (e.g., GPT-4) hosted on European Azure servers with enterprise-grade safeguards. Offers the highest response quality and advanced reasoning.
- **On-premise** (Experimental): Uses an open-source model running entirely on your institution's own infrastructure. No data leaves the university, but response quality may be lower.
- **No AI**: Opt out of AI-powered features entirely. You can change this at any time.

<Image src={irisAiExperienceSelection} alt="AI Experience Selection" size={ImageSize.large} caption={"Choose between Cloud, On-premise, or No AI"}></Image>

You can change this setting at any time from within Iris.

### Iris Memory

When choosing your AI experience, you can also enable **Iris Memory**. When enabled, Iris remembers your learning preferences and past interactions across conversations to provide more personalized support over time. You can disable this at any time.

When memory is active, you will see a small brain icon on messages indicating how many memories were used or created during that interaction. You can view and manage all stored memories in your learner profile settings.

## Where to Find Iris

Iris is available in several places, each tailored to a specific learning context:

### Course Chat

The course chat is your general-purpose assistant for a course. Open it from the course sidebar. Iris can answer questions about lecture content, course topics, and general programming concepts. It has access to all lectures and course materials.

<Image src={irisCourseChat} alt="Iris Course Chat" size={ImageSize.large} caption={"Iris course chat with conversation history and follow-up suggestions"}></Image>

### Exercise and Lecture Chat

When working on an exercise or viewing a lecture, click the floating Iris icon in the bottom right corner to open a chat widget. In this mode, Iris automatically knows your context:

- **Programming exercises:** Iris can reference your problem statement, code, build logs, and test results — no copy-pasting needed. This makes it particularly effective at helping you debug issues or understand what a failing test expects.
- **Text exercises:** Iris can see your current submission and the problem statement.
- **Lectures:** Iris references the specific lecture slides and materials to provide relevant answers.

<Image src={irisExerciseChat} alt="Iris Exercise Chat" size={ImageSize.large} caption={"Iris chat widget within a programming exercise"}></Image>

## How Iris Helps You Learn

### Context-Aware Responses

Unlike general-purpose AI chatbots, Iris automatically sees the context you are working in. In an exercise, it reads your code and build output. In a lecture, it references the slides. This means you can ask focused questions like _"Why is my sorting algorithm failing?"_ without having to explain your entire setup.

### Citations

When Iris references specific course materials, it includes **citations** — small numbered markers in its responses. Hover over a citation to see which lecture slide, video segment, or FAQ entry it came from. This lets you verify Iris's answers and dive deeper into the source material.

### Follow-Up Suggestions

After each response, Iris may suggest follow-up questions as clickable buttons below the message. These are designed to help you explore a topic further or clarify related concepts. Click a suggestion to send it as your next message.

### Proactive Hints

In some exercises, Iris may proactively offer a hint if it detects you might be stuck — for example, after repeated build failures. These hints appear as chat messages and follow the same guided-learning approach.

## What to Expect

- **Guide, Not Solve:** Iris does not provide complete solutions. It nudges you in the right direction with hints and explanations so you build genuine understanding.
- **Stay on Topic:** Iris answers questions about course content and programming topics. It will not handle off-topic requests or organizational questions (exam dates, room assignments, etc.).
- **Own Your Work:** Use Iris as a learning aid while maintaining academic integrity. Your submissions must reflect your own understanding and effort.
- **Quality May Vary:** If you chose the on-premise model, responses may be less detailed or accurate than the cloud option. Try rephrasing your question if a response isn't helpful.

## Interacting with Iris

- **Send a message:** Type your question and press Enter. Use Shift+Enter for multi-line messages.
- **Rate responses:** Use the thumbs up/down buttons on Iris's messages. Your feedback directly helps improve Iris over time.
- **Copy responses:** Click the copy icon on any message to copy its text.
- **Chat history:** In the course chat, the sidebar on the left shows all your past conversations, organized by date. You can search through them or start a new chat at any time.
- **New chat:** Click the pen icon to start a fresh conversation. Each chat session is independent.

## Usage Limits

Your instructor may configure a maximum number of Iris interactions per time period (e.g., 20 messages per 24 hours). If a limit is set, you will see a counter in the chat header showing your current usage. When you reach the limit, you will need to wait for the time window to reset before sending new messages.

## Privacy and Data

- **Cloud AI:** Your interactions are processed on European Azure servers with enterprise-grade data protection. No data is used for model training.
- **On-premise AI:** All processing happens on your institution's own infrastructure. No data leaves the university network.

In both cases, review your institution's privacy policy for full details. You can switch between options or disable AI entirely at any time.
